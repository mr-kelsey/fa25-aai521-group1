About Image Quality Metrics

PSNR (Peak Signal-to-Noise Ratio): Measures the ratio between the maximum possible power of a signal and the power of corrupting noise. Higher values indicate better quality (typically 30-50 dB is considered good).

SSIM (Structural Similarity Index Measure): Measures the similarity between two images, considering luminance, contrast, and structure. Values range from -1 to 1, where 1 indicates perfect similarity.
